Sunday School Plan: Third Sunday after Pentecost

Season After Pentecost (Green)
Scripture: Matthew 9:35–10:8, (9–23)
Theme: Jesus Sends the Twelve, Compassion, Trusting God

Master Materials List

  • Offering plate
  • A large bristol board (pre-divided into 4 sections using marker)
  • Markers or crayons for drawing
  • A phone or speaker to play music for the freeze dance game
  • Extra Sunday School Sheets and crayons/markers (for children who prefer to colour)

1. Introduction and Welcome

5 minutes: Weekend Check-In

Welcome everyone! We are now in the Season after Pentecost. Notice how the colour is green? That reminds us of growing, like a tree or a plant! In this season, we learn how the church grew and how the Holy Spirit helps us grow in God's love. Ask the children how their week was. Let a few share. Then preview the lesson: "Today we are going to learn about a time when Jesus looked at a huge crowd of people and felt so much love for them that he decided he needed his disciples' help to care for them all. But first, let's do our offering."

2. Offering and Prayer

1 minute: Offering

Collect the offering/donations while singing the money dropping song.

1 minute: Prayer over the Offering

"God, thank you for loving us like a good shepherd loves their sheep. Thank you for giving us a job to do: sharing your love and peace with everyone we meet. We offer this gift to you. Help us to hear your words today. In Jesus' name, Amen."

3. The Story

5 minutes: Interactive Story

The Story: (Read this narratively to the children using the iPad App. Encourage them to act it out with you as you read!)

"Jesus traveled all around the cities and villages, teaching and healing every sickness he found. When he saw the huge crowds of people, his heart broke for them. He had so much compassion because they looked tired, harassed, and helpless—like sheep without a shepherd." [Show me your most compassionate face. What does it look like when you care for someone who is hurting?]

"Jesus looked at his disciples and said, 'The harvest is huge, but there are not enough workers to bring it all in.' So he called his twelve disciples together and gave them a very special, powerful job: he gave them the authority to heal the sick and cast out bad spirits." [Pretend you are quickly picking lots and lots of wheat from a huge field! That is what harvesting means.]

"Then Jesus gave them their instructions. He said, 'Go and tell everyone the good news: The kingdom of heaven is near! Heal the sick and give without making anyone pay.' He told them not to bring any money, or an extra bag, or even an extra shirt. They had to trust God to provide everything they needed." [Pat your pockets. Are they empty? Jesus told them to take nothing on their journey and trust God completely!]

"Jesus knew it wouldn't be easy. He said, 'If a town welcomes you, let your peace stay there. But if they don't listen to you, just shake the dust off your feet and keep going.' He told them to be wise, but also innocent, and promised that anyone who endures to the end will be saved." [Lift up your foot and pretend to wipe dust off your shoe. Shake off the dust and keep going!]

4. Questions and Reflections

5 minutes: Ask the children the following questions:

  • Jesus gives instructions to his disciples in this story. What did Jesus tell the disciples to bring, do, and remember? (Answer: He told them to bring NO money or extra clothes. He told them to heal the sick and proclaim the good news. He told them to remember to let their peace stay if welcomed, or shake the dust off if not.)
  • In this season after Pentecost, we learn how to follow Jesus with the Holy Spirit helping us. What can we bring, do, and remember that will help us share about Jesus' love?
  • Jesus gives his disciples the ability to heal people as they share about God's love. Why is it important to both heal and share? (Answer: Because God's love isn't just about words, it's about action and helping people who are hurting.)
  • Jesus tells us to be both wise and innocent. We can think of innocence as not causing harm while doing what is right. When we care for someone who is hurting, why is it important to be both innocent and wise?
  • What rhyme or chant can you come up with to remind us to be wise, innocent, and loving when helping others?

5. Bristol Board Drawing Activity

10 minutes: Drawing the Story Together

Transition: "Let's draw out the four big parts of today's story on the bristol board!"

  • Section 1 (Compassion): Have 1-2 children draw Jesus with a crowd of people looking tired like sheep. Ask: "What does compassion mean?"
  • Section 2 (The Twelve): Have 1-2 children draw the twelve disciples gathering around Jesus. Ask: "Why did Jesus need their help?"
  • Section 3 (Empty Pockets): Have 1-2 children draw the disciples walking with empty pockets and no bags. Ask: "Who were they trusting to take care of them?"
  • Section 4 (Shaking Dust): Have the rest draw someone wiping dust off their sandal at a closed door. Ask: "What did Jesus say to do if someone doesn't welcome you?"

6. Reading the Scripture

2 minutes: Matthew 9:35–10:8

Transition: "Now let's read the actual words from the Gospel of Matthew about Jesus sending out the twelve!"

Then Jesus went about all the cities and villages, teaching in their synagogues and proclaiming the good news of the kingdom and curing every disease and every sickness. When he saw the crowds, he had compassion for them because they were harassed and helpless, like sheep without a shepherd. Then he said to his disciples, "The harvest is plentiful, but the laborers are few; therefore ask the Lord of the harvest to send out laborers into his harvest."

Then Jesus summoned his twelve disciples and gave them authority over unclean spirits, to cast them out, and to cure every disease and every sickness. [...]

These twelve Jesus sent out with the following instructions: "Do not take a road leading to gentiles, and do not enter a Samaritan town, but go rather to the lost sheep of the house of Israel. As you go, proclaim the good news, 'The kingdom of heaven has come near.' Cure the sick; raise the dead; cleanse those with a skin disease; cast out demons. You received without payment; give without payment."

7. Game / Activity

20 minutes: "Jesus Says" Freeze Dance

(Note: Any children who don't want to participate can colour using the extra Sunday School sheets!)

Intro: "We are playing freeze dance with words from today's story! When I say 'Jesus says' before the word, you do the action. If I just say the word without it, you stay still. If you move, you are out for that round but back in the next one!"

The 5 Calls:

  • "Compassion!" Place both hands over your heart and give a warm smile.
  • "Harvest!" Pretend you are picking wheat from a field as fast as you can.
  • "Go!" Don't freeze! March in place while patting your empty pockets.
  • "Heal!" Gently place your hand on the shoulder of the person closest to you.
  • "Dust!" Lift one foot and pretend to wipe dust off your shoe.

How to Play (Jesus Says rules):

  1. Play upbeat music and let the children dance freely around the room.
  2. Stop the music. Only do the action if you say "Jesus says..." first. If you just say the word, they should stay still.
  3. Anyone who does the action without the prompt is out for that round and back in the next.
  4. Mix up speed, order, and whether you use the prompt to keep them guessing.
  5. At the end, ask: "Which of these actions was the hardest to remember?"

8. Wrap Up and Prayer

5 minutes: Wrap Up

  • Wrap up: Remind the children that Jesus didn't just tell the disciples to talk about God's love, he told them to SHOW it by healing the sick and caring for people. He also told them to trust God entirely, not even packing a bag for the trip. We might not be traveling to ancient towns with no money, but we can still show compassion to people around us and trust God to help us.
  • Closing Prayer: "God, thank you for having compassion on us. Thank you for giving us the important job of sharing your love and peace. Help us to be wise, innocent, and kind. Help us to trust you even when we don't have everything figured out. We pray this in Jesus' name, Amen."
