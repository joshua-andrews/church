Jesus traveled all around the cities and villages, teaching and healing every sickness he found. When he saw the huge crowds of people, his heart broke for them. He had so much compassion because they looked tired, harassed, and helpless—like sheep without a shepherd.
* Show me your most compassionate face. What does it look like when you care for someone who is hurting?
Jesus looked at his disciples and said, "The harvest is huge, but there are not enough workers to bring it all in." So he called his twelve disciples together and gave them a very special, powerful job: he gave them the authority to heal the sick and cast out bad spirits.
* Pretend you are quickly picking lots and lots of wheat from a huge field! That is what harvesting means.
Then Jesus gave them their instructions. He said, "Go and tell everyone the good news: The kingdom of heaven is near! Heal the sick and give without making anyone pay." He told them not to bring any money, or an extra bag, or even an extra shirt. They had to trust God to provide everything they needed.
* Pat your pockets. Are they empty? Jesus told them to take nothing on their journey and trust God completely!
Jesus knew it wouldn't be easy. He said, "If a town welcomes you, let your peace stay there. But if they don't listen to you, just shake the dust off your feet and keep going." He told them to be wise, but also innocent, and promised that anyone who endures to the end will be saved.
* Lift up your foot and pretend to wipe dust off your shoe. Shake off the dust and keep going!
