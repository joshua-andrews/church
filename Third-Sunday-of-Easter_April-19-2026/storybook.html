Just weeks after Easter morning, Jesus' friend Peter stood up in front of thousands of people in Jerusalem. He raised his voice and spoke with total confidence: "Everyone in Israel needs to know this. God has made Jesus both Lord and Messiah!"
* Repeat after me in a loud voice: God has made Jesus both Lord and Messiah!
When the crowd heard Peter's words, they felt something deep inside their chests. The Bible says they were "cut to the heart." That means it hit them like a sudden, heavy feeling in your stomach when you realize you have done something wrong and you cannot take it back. They cried out: "Brothers! What should we do?" Peter answered: "Repent and be baptized. Your sins will be forgiven and you will receive the gift of the Holy Spirit."
* Who can show me your really sad face?
Then Peter said the most amazing thing of all: "This promise is for you. And for your children. And for everyone who is far away. Absolutely everyone that God calls to himself." People ran to the water to be baptized, to say yes to God's promise and yes to belonging to God's family.
* Point to yourself. Point to the person next to you. Then point to me. Point, point, point, point. It is for everyone.
And that day, about three thousand people were welcomed into God's family. Three thousand! In one single day! The first Easter church grew because everyone was welcomed in. And that same promise is still for us today, for you and for me, and for everyone near and far.
