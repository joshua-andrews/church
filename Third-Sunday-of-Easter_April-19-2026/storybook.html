Just weeks after Easter, Jesus' friend Peter stood up in front of thousands of people in Jerusalem and spoke with a big, bold voice: 'Everyone in Israel needs to know — God has made Jesus both Lord and Messiah!' * Stand up tall and put on your most confident face — like Peter! It was one of the bravest things anyone had ever done.
When the people heard Peter's words, they felt it right in their chests. The Bible says they were 'cut to the heart' — a deep, heavy sadness. They cried out: 'Brothers! What should we do?' * Show me what it looks like when you feel really sad or sorry about something. Peter answered: 'Repent and be baptized — every single one of you. Your sins will be forgiven, and you will receive the gift of the Holy Spirit.'
Then Peter said the most amazing thing: 'This promise is for YOU. And for your CHILDREN. And for everyone who is far away — absolutely everyone that God calls.' * Point to yourself — "for YOU." Point to your neighbour — "for them." Spread your arms wide — "for EVERYONE!" People ran to the water to be baptized — to say YES to God's promise and YES to belonging to God's family.
And that day — that one single day — about three thousand people were welcomed into the church family. * Throw your hands in the air and cheer — THREE THOUSAND PEOPLE! The first Easter church grew because everyone was welcomed in. And that same promise is still for us today — for you, for me, and for everyone, near and far.
