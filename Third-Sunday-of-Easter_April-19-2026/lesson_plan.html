Sunday School Plan: Third Sunday of Easter

Christ is Risen! Christ is Risen, Indeed!
Scripture: Acts 2:14a, 36-41
Theme: God's Promise is for Everyone. Welcome to the Family!

Master Materials List

  • Offering plate
  • A large bristol board (pre-divided into 4 sections using marker)
  • Markers or crayons for drawing
  • A phone or speaker to play music for the freeze dance game
  • Extra Sunday School Sheets and crayons/markers (for children who don't want to participate in the game and prefer to color)

1. Introduction and Welcome

5 minutes: Weekend Check-In

Welcome everyone back! Ask the children if there was a PA day this week and if anyone did anything exciting over the long weekend. Let them share briefly. Then give a quick preview of what's coming: "Today we are going to hear a story about Peter and the very first Easter church. Thousands of people, all in one place, and something incredible happens. But first, before we get into it, let's do our offering!"

2. Offering and Prayer

1 minute: Offering

Collect the offering/donations while singing the money dropping song.

1 minute: Prayer over the Offering

"Risen Christ, thank you for bringing us together again today. We offer this gift and we offer ourselves. Thank you that your promise is for all of us, every single one. Help us to hear your word and to know that we belong to your family. Amen."

3. The Story

5 minutes: Interactive Story

The Story: (Read this narratively to the children using the iPad App. Encourage them to act it out with you as you read!)

"Just a few weeks after Jesus rose from the dead on Easter morning, something incredible happened in Jerusalem. Jesus' closest friend, a fisherman named Peter, stood up in front of an enormous crowd. Imagine thousands and thousands of people, all packed together in one place. And Peter started to speak!

Peter looked out at the crowd and said with a big, bold voice: 'Everyone in Israel needs to know this with certainty. God has made Jesus both Lord and Messiah. This is the Jesus that you crucified.' [Repeat after me in a loud voice: God has made Jesus both Lord and Messiah!]

When the people in the crowd heard Peter's words, they felt something deep inside their chests. The Bible says they were "cut to the heart." That means it hit them like a sudden, heavy feeling in your stomach when you realize you have done something wrong and you cannot take it back. They called out to Peter: "Brothers! What should we do?" [Who can show me your really sad face?]

Peter answered: "Repent and be baptized, every single one of you, in the name of Jesus Christ. Your sins will be forgiven, and you will receive the gift of the Holy Spirit." Then he said the most amazing thing of all: "This promise is for you. And for your children. And for everyone who is far away. Absolutely everyone that God calls." [Point to yourself. Point to the person next to you. Then point to me. Point, point, point, point. It is for everyone.]

And that day, the Bible tells us, about three thousand people were baptized and added to the church family. Three thousand! In one single day! The church was growing, and everyone was welcomed in."

4. Questions and Reflections

5 minutes: Ask the children the following questions:

  • When the crowd heard Peter speak, the Bible says they were "cut to the heart." Have you ever heard something that hit you really hard right in the chest? What was it?
  • When the crowd asked "what should we do?", what did Peter tell them to do — and what does each of those things actually mean? (Answer: Repent means to turn around and start fresh. Be baptized means water is poured over you as a symbol that your sins are forgiven, you belong to God's family, and you receive the Holy Spirit.)
  • Peter told the crowd what to DO, not just what to believe. What is one thing you could do differently this week because of today's story?

5. Bristol Board Drawing Activity

10 minutes: Drawing the Story Together

Transition: "In our story today, the church went from a small group of friends to three thousand people in one day! Let's draw the whole story on the bristol board!"

  • Section 1 (Peter Speaks Boldly): Have 1-2 children draw Peter standing on a platform or steps with his arm raised, speaking to a crowd. Ask: "What was Peter trying to tell the crowd?"
  • Section 2 (Cut to the Heart): Have 1-2 children draw people in the crowd with their hands over their hearts looking sad and asking "What should we do?" Ask: "How did the crowd feel when they heard Peter's words?"
  • Section 3 (Baptized in the River): Have 1-2 children draw people being baptized, someone pouring water and people smiling. Ask: "What did Peter tell them to do?"
  • Section 4 (Three Thousand Join!): Have the rest draw a massive, joyful crowd of diverse people celebrating with their arms in the air. Ask: "Who did Peter say God's promise was for?"

6. Reading the Scripture

2 minutes: Acts 2:14a, 36-41

Transition: "Now that we have heard the story and drawn it, let's read the actual words from the Bible, from the book of Acts!"

"But Peter, standing with the eleven, raised his voice and addressed them, 'Therefore let the entire house of Israel know with certainty that God has made him both Lord and Messiah, this Jesus whom you crucified.'

Now when they heard this, they were cut to the heart and said to Peter and to the other apostles, 'Brothers, what should we do?' Peter said to them, 'Repent and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ so that your sins may be forgiven, and you will receive the gift of the Holy Spirit.

For the promise is for you, for your children, and for all who are far away, everyone whom the Lord our God calls to him.'

So those who welcomed his message were baptized, and that day about three thousand persons were added."

7. Game / Activity

20 minutes: "Peter's Crowd" Freeze Dance

(Note: Any children who don't want to participate can colour using the extra Sunday School sheets!)

Intro: "We're going to play a freeze dance game. Every time the music stops, I'm going to call out a word from our story today, and you have to freeze doing the right action. But here's the trick: you only do the action if I say 'Peter says' first. If I just say the word on its own, stay still! Let's practice."

The 5 Calls:

  • "Heart!" Freeze and put both hands over your heart with an emotional expression.
  • "Repent!" Freeze and press your hands together in prayer position, close your eyes, and bow your head.
  • "Baptize!" Freeze with your arms stretched wide open and your face tilted up to the sky.
  • "Far away!" Freeze and point dramatically off into the distance.
  • "Three thousand!" Don't freeze! Run and give as many high fives as you can before the music starts again.

How to Play (Peter Says rules):

  1. Play upbeat music and let the children dance freely around the room.
  2. Stop the music and call out a word. Only if you say "Peter says..." first should the kids do the action. If you just say the word on its own, they should stay still.
  3. Anyone who does the action without "Peter says" is out for that round. Let them know they are back in next round so they stay engaged and keep watching.
  4. Mix up the calls, speed, and whether you say "Peter says" to keep them on their toes.
  5. At the end, ask: "Can anyone remember all five words from today's story?" See if the group can recall all five as a recap.

8. Wrap Up and Prayer

5 minutes: Wrap Up

  • Wrap up: Remind the children that the very first Easter church grew because people welcomed one another in. Every single person, near or far, young or old, is part of the promise. We are part of that promise too!
  • Closing Prayer: "God, thank you for the gift of Easter and for the promise that is for all of us. Thank you that we belong to your family and that no one has to feel left out. Help us to be a church that welcomes everyone in with open arms, just like Peter did. In Jesus' name, Amen."
