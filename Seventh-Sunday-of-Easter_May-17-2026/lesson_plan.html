Sunday School Plan: Seventh Sunday of Easter

Christ is Risen! Christ is Risen, Indeed!
Scripture: Acts 1:6-14
Theme: Jesus Goes to Heaven and Gives Us a Job

Master Materials List

  • Offering plate
  • A large bristol board (pre-divided into 4 sections using marker)
  • Markers or crayons for drawing
  • A phone or speaker to play music for the freeze dance game
  • Extra Sunday School Sheets and crayons/markers (for children who prefer to colour)

1. Introduction and Welcome

5 minutes: Weekend Check-In

Welcome everyone back! Ask the children how their week was and if anything fun or exciting happened. Let a few share. Then preview what is coming: "Today we are going to hear one of the most amazing stories in the whole Bible. Jesus is about to leave his friends, but before he goes, he gives them a really important job. And then something incredible happens. But first, let's do our offering."

2. Offering and Prayer

1 minute: Offering

Collect the offering/donations while singing the money dropping song.

1 minute: Prayer over the Offering

"Risen Christ, thank you for the time you spent with your disciples after Easter. Thank you for not leaving them without a purpose. We offer this gift and we offer ourselves to you. Help us to be your witnesses today. Amen."

3. The Story

5 minutes: Interactive Story

The Story: (Read this narratively to the children using the iPad App. Encourage them to act it out with you as you read!)

"After Jesus rose from the dead, he spent time with his disciples for forty days, teaching them and eating with them. One day, they all gathered together on a hilltop called the Mount of Olives. The disciples had a big question. They asked Jesus: 'Lord, is this the time when you will finally make everything right for Israel?' [Can you lean in and look really curious, like you are about to ask someone a big question?]

Jesus looked at his friends and said something they did not expect. He said: 'It is not for you to know when. That is up to God. But here is what I can tell you: you will receive power when the Holy Spirit comes to you. And you will be my witnesses in Jerusalem, in all of Judea, in Samaria, and all the way to the ends of the earth.' [Cup your hands around your mouth like a megaphone. You are a witness! Tell the person next to you: "To the ends of the earth!"]

And then, right before their eyes, Jesus was lifted up into the sky. A cloud came and took him out of their sight. The disciples just stood there, staring up, amazed. Then two men in bright white robes appeared beside them and said: 'Why are you standing here looking up? This same Jesus will come back the same way you saw him go.' [Look up at the ceiling. Shield your eyes with your hand like you are staring into bright light.]

So the disciples walked back to Jerusalem and went to the upper room where they were staying. Peter, John, James, Andrew, and all the others. The women were there too, including Mary, Jesus' mother. And together, they did exactly what they knew how to do. They prayed. They waited. And they trusted that what Jesus promised would come true." [Close your eyes, fold your hands, and take one deep breath. That is what waiting and trusting looks like.]

4. Questions and Reflections

5 minutes: Ask the children the following questions:

  • The disciples wanted to know exactly when God would fix everything. Jesus told them it was not for them to know. Can you tell me about a time when you really wanted to know how something would turn out, but you just had to wait? What did that feel like?
  • Instead of answering their question, Jesus gave the disciples a job. He said: "You will be my witnesses." A witness is someone who tells the truth about what they have seen. What does it mean to be a witness for Jesus today? (Answer: It means living in a way that shows others what Jesus is like, telling the truth, being kind, standing up for people, and sharing what you believe.)
  • After Jesus left, the disciples did not panic. They went to a room, gathered together, and prayed. When something big or uncertain happens in your life, what do you do? Who do you gather with?

5. Bristol Board Drawing Activity

10 minutes: Drawing the Story Together

Transition: "This story has four really powerful moments. Let's draw each one on the bristol board!"

  • Section 1 (The Big Question): Have 1-2 children draw the disciples gathered around Jesus on the hilltop, asking their question. Ask: "What did the disciples want to know?"
  • Section 2 (The Mission): Have 1-2 children draw Jesus with arms wide, sending the disciples out. Ask: "What job did Jesus give them?"
  • Section 3 (The Ascension): Have 1-2 children draw Jesus being lifted into the sky with a cloud and the disciples looking up. Ask: "What happened when Jesus left? Who appeared?"
  • Section 4 (The Upper Room): Have the rest draw a group of people in a room, praying together by candlelight. Ask: "What did the disciples do after Jesus went to heaven?"

6. Reading the Scripture

2 minutes: Acts 1:6-14

Transition: "Now that we have heard the story and drawn it, let's read the actual words from the book of Acts!"

So when they had come together, they asked him, "Lord, is this the time when you will restore the kingdom to Israel?" He replied, "It is not for you to know the times or periods that God has set by his own authority. But you will receive power when the Holy Spirit has come upon you, and you will be my witnesses in Jerusalem, in all Judea and Samaria, and to the ends of the earth."

When he had said this, as they were watching, he was lifted up, and a cloud took him out of their sight. While he was going and they were gazing up toward heaven, suddenly two men in white robes stood by them. They said, "Men of Galilee, why do you stand looking up toward heaven? This Jesus, who has been taken up from you into heaven, will come in the same way as you saw him go into heaven."

Then they returned to Jerusalem from the mount called Olivet, which is near Jerusalem, a Sabbath day's journey away. When they had entered the city, they went to the room upstairs where they were staying: Peter, and John, and James, and Andrew, Philip and Thomas, Bartholomew and Matthew, James son of Alphaeus, and Simon the Zealot, and Judas son of James. All these were constantly devoting themselves to prayer, together with certain women, including Mary the mother of Jesus, as well as his brothers.

7. Game / Activity

20 minutes: "Jesus Says" Freeze Dance

(Note: Any children who don't want to participate can colour using the extra Sunday School sheets!)

Intro: "We are playing freeze dance again with brand new words from today's story! When I say 'Jesus says' before the word, you do the action. If I just say the word without it, you stay still. If you move, you are out for that round but back in the next one!"

The 5 Calls:

  • "Question!" Lean in with one hand cupped to your ear, looking super curious.
  • "Witness!" Cup both hands around your mouth like a megaphone.
  • "Heaven!" Look up and shield your eyes with your hand like you are staring into bright light.
  • "Pray!" Press your hands together, close your eyes, and bow your head.
  • "Go!" Don't freeze! Run and give everyone around you a high five. You are being sent out to the ends of the earth!

How to Play (Jesus Says rules):

  1. Play upbeat music and let the children dance freely around the room.
  2. Stop the music. Only do the action if you say "Jesus says..." first. If you just say the word, they should stay still.
  3. Anyone who does the action without the prompt is out for that round and back in the next.
  4. Mix up speed, order, and whether you use the prompt to keep them guessing.
  5. At the end, ask: "Can anyone remember all five words from today's story?"

8. Wrap Up and Prayer

5 minutes: Wrap Up

  • Wrap up: Remind the children that even when the disciples did not get the answer they wanted, Jesus gave them something better: a purpose. He told them to be his witnesses, to tell the truth about what they had seen. And when they did not know what to do next, they gathered together and prayed. We can do the same thing.
  • Closing Prayer: "Jesus, thank you for giving us a job to do. Thank you that even when we do not know what comes next, we can trust you. Help us to be your witnesses this week, to tell the truth, to be brave, and to gather together when we need each other. In your name, Amen."
