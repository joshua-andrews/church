Have you ever felt really scared? Maybe in the dark, or when something felt wrong and you did not know what to do? A long time ago, a young man named David wrote a prayer to God from that exact feeling. That prayer became Psalm 31. David cried out: "God, I need you. I need somewhere safe to go."
* Can you make yourself as small as possible, like you are hiding?
David looked up and prayed: "In you, O Lord, I seek refuge. Be a rock of refuge for me, a strong fortress to save me. You are my rock and my fortress." A refuge is a place where you are completely safe, protected, and held. David believed that God could be that place.
* Raise both hands up to the sky like you are calling out to God.
A rock does not move. A fortress does not fall. When David called God a rock and a fortress, he was saying: God is the one thing that will never shift, never crumble, never let me down. No matter what is happening outside, inside God's protection there is peace.
* Freeze completely still. Plant your feet. Be the rock.
Then David said the most beautiful thing: "Into your hand I commit my spirit." That means: God, I trust you completely with everything I am. This is the same prayer Jesus prayed on the cross. And because of Easter, we know that those hands are safe hands. God holds us, and God does not let go.
* Open both hands, palms up. Close your eyes. That is what trust looks like.
