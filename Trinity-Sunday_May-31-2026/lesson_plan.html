Sunday School Plan: Trinity Sunday

First Sunday after Pentecost
Scripture: Matthew 28:16-20
Theme: Go and Make Disciples, I Am With You Always

Master Materials List

  • Offering plate
  • A large bristol board (pre-divided into 4 sections using marker)
  • Markers or crayons for drawing
  • A phone or speaker to play music for the freeze dance game
  • Extra Sunday School Sheets and crayons/markers (for children who prefer to colour)

1. Introduction and Welcome

5 minutes: Weekend Check-In

Welcome everyone back! Ask the children how their week was and if anything fun or exciting happened. Let a few share. Then preview what is coming: "Today is a really special Sunday called Trinity Sunday. We are going to hear the very last thing Jesus ever said to his disciples before he left. It is one of the most important things in the whole Bible. He gave them a job, and he made them a promise. But first, let's do our offering."

2. Offering and Prayer

1 minute: Offering

Collect the offering/donations while singing the money dropping song.

1 minute: Prayer over the Offering

"God, thank you for sending Jesus to show us who you are. Thank you for the Holy Spirit who is with us right now. We offer this gift and we offer ourselves to you. Help us to hear your words today. In Jesus' name, Amen."

3. The Story

5 minutes: Interactive Story

The Story: (Read this narratively to the children using the iPad App. Encourage them to act it out with you as you read!)

"After Jesus rose from the dead, he told his eleven disciples to meet him on a mountain in Galilee. When they got there and saw him, they fell to their knees and worshipped him. But some of them were not sure. They doubted. Even after everything they had seen, some of them still were not certain." [Have you ever not been sure about something even when everyone else seemed sure? Shrug your shoulders and make your most confused face.]

"Jesus came close to them and said something incredible: 'All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me.' That means there is nothing and no one more powerful than Jesus. Not in the sky, not on the ground, not anywhere. All of it belongs to him." [Repeat after me in your biggest voice: "ALL authority in heaven and on earth!"]

"Then Jesus gave them the most important job in history. He said: 'Go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you.' He was sending them out to tell everyone, everywhere, about God." [Point as far as you can. Point to the door. Point to the window. Point everywhere! That is how far Jesus wants the message to go.]

"And then Jesus said the last and most beautiful thing: 'And remember, I am with you always, to the end of the age.' He was not sending them out alone. He promised to be with them every single day, no matter what. And that same promise is for us too. Jesus is with us always." [Wrap your arms around yourself in the biggest hug you can. That is what "I am with you always" feels like.]

4. Questions and Reflections

5 minutes: Ask the children the following questions:

  • Some of the disciples worshipped Jesus, but some doubted. Is it okay to have doubts? Can you tell me about a time you were not sure about something? (Answer: Yes, it is okay. Even the people who saw Jesus in person had doubts. Having questions does not mean you do not believe. It means you are thinking.)
  • Jesus said to baptize people "in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit." Today is Trinity Sunday, which is when we celebrate that God is three in one: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. What do you think each one does? (Answer: The Father created everything. The Son, Jesus, came to show us what God is like. The Holy Spirit is God with us right now, guiding us and giving us strength.)
  • The very last thing Jesus said was "I am with you always." When is a time you really needed to know that someone was with you? What did that feel like?

5. Bristol Board Drawing Activity

10 minutes: Drawing the Story Together

Transition: "This story has four big moments. Let's draw each one on the bristol board!"

  • Section 1 (The Mountain): Have 1-2 children draw the disciples climbing a mountain and seeing Jesus at the top. Ask: "How do you think they felt when they saw him?"
  • Section 2 (All Authority): Have 1-2 children draw Jesus standing with arms wide open, light behind him. Ask: "What does it mean that all authority belongs to Jesus?"
  • Section 3 (Go to All Nations): Have 1-2 children draw people going out in all directions from the mountain. Ask: "Where did Jesus tell them to go?"
  • Section 4 (I Am With You Always): Have the rest draw Jesus surrounded by a warm glow with his disciples close. Ask: "What was the last thing Jesus promised?"

6. Reading the Scripture

2 minutes: Matthew 28:16-20

Transition: "Now that we have heard the story and drawn it, let's read the actual words from the Gospel of Matthew!"

Now the eleven disciples went to Galilee, to the mountain to which Jesus had directed them. When they saw him, they worshiped him; but some doubted.

And Jesus came and said to them, "All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me. Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything that I have commanded you.

And remember, I am with you always, to the end of the age."

7. Game / Activity

20 minutes: "Jesus Says" Freeze Dance

(Note: Any children who don't want to participate can colour using the extra Sunday School sheets!)

Intro: "We are playing freeze dance with brand new words from today's story! When I say 'Jesus says' before the word, you do the action. If I just say the word without it, you stay still. If you move, you are out for that round but back in the next one!"

The 5 Calls:

  • "Doubt!" Shrug your shoulders and make your most confused face.
  • "Authority!" Stand as tall as you can and flex your arms like you are the strongest person in the room.
  • "Go!" Don't freeze! Point everywhere, then run and give everyone around you a high five.
  • "Baptize!" Motion like you are gently pouring water over your own head.
  • "Always!" Wrap your arms around yourself in the biggest hug. Jesus is with you always.

How to Play (Jesus Says rules):

  1. Play upbeat music and let the children dance freely around the room.
  2. Stop the music. Only do the action if you say "Jesus says..." first. If you just say the word, they should stay still.
  3. Anyone who does the action without the prompt is out for that round and back in the next.
  4. Mix up speed, order, and whether you use the prompt to keep them guessing.
  5. At the end, ask: "Can anyone remember all five words from today's story?"

8. Wrap Up and Prayer

5 minutes: Wrap Up

  • Wrap up: Remind the children that the very last words Jesus said to his disciples were not a warning or a rule. They were a promise: "I am with you always." That is for us too. Wherever we go this week, whatever we face, Jesus promised to be right there with us. And today, on Trinity Sunday, we celebrate that God is with us in three ways: as the Father who made us, the Son who showed us, and the Holy Spirit who stays with us.
  • Closing Prayer: "Jesus, thank you for your promise. Thank you that you are with us always, to the end of the age. Help us to go and share your love this week. Help us to be brave even when we doubt. We trust you, Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. Amen."
